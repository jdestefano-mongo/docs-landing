Login and Access Changes to MongoDB Services

Publish Date: March 25th, 2019

On April XXth, 2019 we are launching a new unified login experience. The new login gives you access to all MongoDB services (MongoDB Atlas, MongoDB Cloud Manager, Support, JIRA, and MongoDB University) with a single identity that allows you to move between services seamlessly without needing to reauthenticate.

What does this mean?

When you log in to a MongoDB service, you will be redirected to a new unified login screen, as seen below. You will authenticate using the same credentials you used in the past to access MongoDB services.

How am I affected?

MongoDB University uses a unique set of user credentials, separate from the other MongoDB services. If you would like to merge your University account with your account for other MongoDB services, please log in to MongoDB University and change your email to match your account for other MongoDB services. If you would prefer to keep them separate, no further action is necessary.

In the future, you will be able to log in using different authentication providers such as Google Sign-In or SSO providers like Okta, Ping, and others. Customers using supported SSO providers will be able to federate their identity to MongoDB.

This document will be updated with the progress of the project. Last updated on March 18, 2019.
